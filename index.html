About Me

Robotics Systems Engineer & Security Researcher

I am a Master of Applied Science student in Computer Engineering at the University of Waterloo, supervised by Dr. Sebastian Fischmeister in the Real-Time Embedded Systems Lab. My research focuses on the safety, security, and runtime verification of autonomous and cyber-physical systems, with the goal of developing resilient control architectures for robotics and intelligent machines.

My work explores how secure control, side-channel analysis, and anomaly detection can be integrated to safeguard autonomous platforms against cyber and physical threats. I am particularly interested in building secure infrastructure for autonomous systems — ensuring that autonomous vehicles and robots can operate safely, verifiably, and interoperably in complex environments.

Previously, I have worked as a Robot Platform Software Engineer at OTTO Motors by Rockwell Automation (Clearpath Inc.), where I contributed to the development and maintenance of autonomous mobile robot platform software. This included the robot embedded systems and ROS-based control of the platform. Here I gained experience in designing and implementing robot software for motor control, sensor management, battery management and full system integration and testing.

I hold a Bachelor of Applied Science in Computer Engineering from the University of Toronto. During my studies, I completed a year-long internship as an Embedded Software Engineer at Geotab Inc., where I developed multi-threaded Linux-based IoT software and designed a TinyML solution that deployed a neural network on an ARM microcontroller for aggressive driving detection.
