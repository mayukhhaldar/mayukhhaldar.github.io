About Me

Robotics Software Engineer & Systems Security Researcher

I am a Robotics Software Engineer at OTTO Motors by Rockwell Automation Inc. I work on the robot platform, which is the software/firmware interface that focuses on the autonomous robot's hardware control. My role extends beyond autonomy to ensuring the health, safety, and security of the robot's hardware while maintaining full operational autonomy. Achieving these objectives requires innovative approaches to enhance the robot's robustness, which is a challenge I'm deeply passionate about.

I am particularly interested in securing real-time autonomous critical systems to protect against cyber threats and prevent catastrophic failures. To further this goal, I am currently pursuing a Master of Applied Science in Computer Engineering at the University of Waterloo, under the supervision of Dr. Sebastian Fischmeister, at the Real-Time Embedded Systems Lab. My research focuses on the safety and security of real-time systems.

I hold a Bachelor of Applied Science in Computer Engineering from the University of Toronto. My previous experience includes a year-long internship as an Embedded Software Engineer at Geotab Inc., where I contributed to the development of multi-threaded, Linux-based IoT embedded software. Additionally, I initiated and completed a TinyML project, embedding an artificial neural network in an ARM-based microcontroller for detecting aggressive driving.
